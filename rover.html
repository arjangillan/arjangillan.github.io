Mars Rover — University Rover Challenge

Legacy Robotics @ UC Irvine · Assistant Project Manager · May 2025 – Present · Irvine, CA

Legacy Robotics is UC Irvine's first-ever student-built autonomous Mars rover team, competing in the University Rover Challenge — the Mars Society's international competition held at the Mars Desert Research Station in Hanksville, Utah. The team fields FESC (Field Exploration Surface Cruiser), a Mars-analog rover built by 80+ engineers across 5 divisions — Business, Electrical, Mechanical, Science, and Software — to complete four field missions. This cycle 116 universities across 18 countries competed. As Assistant Project Manager I help oversee the full engineering organization and hold the rover to the mechanical and electrical constraints of the competition.

Science Payload — Biosignature Detection My focus area

The rover's primary life-detection method quantifies total soil protein as a biosignature — proteins make up roughly 55% of the dry weight of common soil bacteria, making them strong indicators of past or present life. On the Science team I developed the assay chemistry behind this measurement — the Bradford protein assay and the soil extraction buffer — and authored the chemical safety and lab-handling protocol for running it onboard.

  • Bradford assay — a colorimetric protein assay using Coomassie dye, whose peak absorbance shifts to 595 nm on protein binding; against a BSA standard curve and Beer's Law, measured absorbance converts to protein concentration.
  • Extraction buffer — tested chaotropic Urea/PBS blends to find the formulation that best releases protein from soil into solution ahead of the assay reaction.
  • Onboard safety — wrote the chemical safety and lab-handling protocol governing reagent handling and assay operation on the rover.

Built by the wider Science team: the sample-handling mechanism — servo scooper, peristaltic pumps, spectrophotometer carousel — and the auger drill / cache and NPK secondary sensing.

Full System (team)
  • Chassis & suspension — a weight-optimized aluminum body with isogrid stiffening on a rocker-bogie suspension and differential bar linkage that keeps all six wheels in contact over rough terrain.
  • 6-DOF arm — a cable-and-pulley robotic arm on a planetary-gearbox base, mass-centralized to reduce torque loads, with a four-bar-linkage gripper for parallel, high-friction grasping.
  • Software & autonomy — a ROS2 stack with a behavior-tree mission planner; stereo vision and YOLOv11 for object and ArUco-tag detection, with an EKF fusing vision, GNSS, IMU, and encoder data for SLAM-based navigation.
  • Electrical — four Li-Ion batteries feed a custom Power Distribution Board (regulated 3.3/5/19 V rails) with fuses, reverse-polarity protection, and dual E-stops.
  • Communications — a dual RF link — 900 MHz for resilient telemetry and 5 GHz for high-bandwidth video — using directional and omnidirectional antennas for reliable base-station control.
  • Guidance drone — a lightweight carbon-fiber UAV acting as an aerial relay and second viewpoint for the Delivery mission.
