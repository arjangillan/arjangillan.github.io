Autocorrelogram Spike-Train Analysis Tool

BME 60B Sandbox · Team of 4 · MATLAB · Fall 2025

An interactive MATLAB tool for analyzing neuronal spike-train data, built on a public dataset of 129 neurons from the Buzsáki Lab databank. The autocorrelogram (ACG) is a core neuroscience tool — a histogram of the time intervals between a neuron's own spikes — whose shape reveals firing behavior and helps identify cell type. Our tool computes ACGs, automatically classifies neurons, and compares firing statistics across multiple neurons.

What It Does
  • Autocorrelogram + classification — computes the ACG for a selected neuron and auto-classifies it by the shape near zero-lag — a central trough vs. peak vs. flat maps to regular/fast-spiking, bursting/oscillatory, or undetermined.
  • Multi-neuron comparison — computes and plots mean firing rate, total spike count, and firing regularity (coefficient of variation of inter-spike intervals) across up to five neurons.
  • Interactive menu — a validated menu-driven interface with raster-plot visualization of the first neurons in the dataset.

The project grew out of the team's research backgrounds — I work in a neural-circuit-mapping lab (UCI Xu Lab), where tools like the ACG are used to characterize neuronal firing.
