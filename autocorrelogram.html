Autocorrelogram Spike-Train Analysis Tool

Engineering Data Analysis Project · MATLAB

An interactive MATLAB tool for analyzing neuronal spike-train data, developed on a public dataset of 129 neurons from the Buzsáki Lab databank. The autocorrelogram (ACG), a histogram of the intervals between a neuron's own spikes, is a core neuroscience metric whose shape characterizes firing behavior and aids cell-type identification. The tool computes ACGs, automatically classifies neurons, and compares firing statistics across multiple units.

What It Does
  • Autocorrelogram + classification: computes the ACG for a selected neuron and auto-classifies it by the shape near zero-lag, where a central trough vs. peak vs. flat maps to regular/fast-spiking, bursting/oscillatory, or undetermined.
  • Multi-neuron comparison: computes and plots mean firing rate, total spike count, and firing regularity (coefficient of variation of inter-spike intervals) across up to five neurons.
  • Interactive menu: a validated menu-driven interface with raster-plot visualization of the first neurons in the dataset.
